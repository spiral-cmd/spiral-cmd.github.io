Jürgen Habermas · Theory of Communicative Action, 1981

System & Lebenswelt

Subsystem
System
Self-regulating. Operates independently of shared meaning — steered by impersonal media, not communicative reason.
Steering media
Money Power
Components
Economy
Market exchange steered by money. Strategic action, instrumental rationality.
State
Administrative apparatus steered by power. Bureaucratic coordination.
Mode of action
Strategic / purposive-rational — actors orient to success, not mutual understanding
Background horizon
Lebenswelt
The pre-theoretical background of shared meanings that makes communication possible. Reproduced through communicative action.
Medium
Communicative reason
Components
Culture
Symbolic reproduction — knowledge, interpretation, meaning transmitted across generations.
Society
Legitimate social orders — norms, solidarity, institutional integration.
Personality
Identity formation — competencies for language, action, self-understanding.
Mode of action
Communicative — actors orient to mutual understanding, not strategic success
Colonisation → When System logic invades the Lifeworld — bureaucratisation of care, monetisation of intimate life, juridification of family — communicative action is displaced by strategic rationality. Habermas's core pathology of modernity.
