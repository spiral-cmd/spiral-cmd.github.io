The best wines to buy at Sainsbury's right now

Susy Atkins picks her top 14 from the spring/summer 2026 tasting

Susy Atkins · The Telegraph · 28 May 2026

🍷 Buy six, save 25% — on until 30 May. Stack with Nectar card offers for double discount.

🥂 White Wine

Edizione 789 di Mondelli Famoso Pinot Grigio NV
Italy
£6.25 £5.50 Nectar offer 10.5%
There's more bright fruity flavour here — think pears and green apples — than in many a bland supermarket pinot grigio, and it's very keenly priced. One for casual summer parties and picnics.
TTDGemischter Satz 2025
Weinviertel, Austria
£10 12.5%
A new, worthwhile addition to the TTD range. Gemischter Satz translates to "mixed set" — a field blend of four white grapes. A youthful blend with a merry mix of peach and citrus with a lip-smacking juicy finish.
TTDRheinhessen Sauvignon Blanc 2024
Germany
£8.50 £7.75 Nectar offer 11%
It's rare to come across a German sauvignon blanc. Tangy with plenty of lemon zest and a whistle-clean finish. It even retained a very light spritz. Refreshing and quite the bargain.
TTDDiscovery Marsanne Roussanne 2025
Pays d'Oc, France
£12 £10 Nectar offer
A new wine in the Discovery range featuring lesser-known grapes. A well-made blend of Rhône grapes marsanne and roussanne by the impeccable Jean-Claude Mas. Fresh orange juice aroma, gingery hint, and a little wood-spice. Demands food — grilled chicken or fresh salmon steaks.
TTDAlbariño 2024
Rías Baixas, Spain
£12.50 13%
Albariño fans will enjoy this classy example from the grape's spiritual home in north-west Galicia. A whiff of lychee as well as citrus and peach, with a light saline sprinkle on the finish. A great wine for white fish and seafood.
TTDRiverblock Sauvignon Blanc 2025
Marlborough, New Zealand
£11 12.5%
Good to find a high-quality Marlborough sauvignon blanc which is neither too sweet-sour nor pungent. Brent Marris, one of the region's most renowned winemakers, has delivered this. Fresh grapefruit and passionfruit, with a little tomato vine and currant bush leaf.
Yalumba Y Series Viognier 2023
South Australia
£10.75 13%
Consistently fine. Well-balanced with much of the grape's ripe apricot and peach but enough acidity to keep things refreshing. Not an aperitif — far better matched with roast chicken, fish in creamy sauces, or Indian kormas.

✨ Sparkling Wine

TTDBlanc de Noirs Champagne Brut NV
France
£26 £23 Nectar offer 12%
Love the red berries which peek through richer layers of custard and vanilla in this rather luxurious own-label champagne. 53% pinot noir and 47% pinot meunier. Nicely structured and rounded, finishing very nearly dry. Bravo.

🌸 Rosé Wine

TTDFronton Négrette Rosé 2025
France
£8.75 12.5%
How lovely to pour a rosé that's proper candy pink, not pallid, and generously fruity though dry. Raspberry and rhubarb on the scent, a mid-palate of ripe strawberry, then a little salt and white pepper at the end. Perfect for charcuterie, including spicy salami and chorizo.

🍷 Red Wine

TTDBeaujolais Supérieur 2023
France
£12.50 £10.50 Nectar offer 12.5%
Wafting a summery aroma of ripe strawberries and red cherries, this is a juicy Beaujolais with light tannins and a blackcurrant seam. No oak ageing to get in the way. Would be banging with bangers, barbecued burgers and pork.
TTDSicilian Nero d'Avola 2024
Italy
£8.50 £7.75 Nectar offer 13%
You can taste the hot Sicilian sun in the ripeness of the local grape nero d'avola. Plenty of black cherry, damson and Victoria plum jam flavours without turning too sweet. Plump and soft with rounded tannins. Delicious with lamb tagine or a rich ragu sauce.
Maison Fortant Reserve Syrah 2024
Southern France
£5.15 11.5%
Surprising the abv is so light when the syrah grapes come from such a hot spot. But this works nonetheless and is one heck of a bargain, with brambly ripeness and a bit of tannin, a spicy twist to its tail. A decent party red.
Hautes Terres Côtes du Rhône NV
France
£5.29 12.5%
Another great buy from southern France. A blend of grenache, syrah and carignan, unoaked and with inviting blackberry and damson flavours. Enough structure to take on grilled pork, beef and barbecued peppers.
TTDHigh Altitude Malbec 2023
Mendoza, Argentina
£13.75 13.5%
High altitude vineyards often produce fruit with fresher, more subtle flavours. All the ripe black cherry expected but not jammy, with lifted, clean acidity and good balance. An appealing dark chocolate seam. Grilled steak wine, of course.

🚫 One to Swerve

TTDProsecco Rosé Brut Millesimato 2025
Italy
£10 10.5%
A pink prosecco that tastes too sweet and confected, like boiled sweets and marshmallows.
