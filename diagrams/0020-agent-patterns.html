Agent Patterns

3 tiers · 15 patterns · one decision before every build
Tier 1 Single-Agent 4 patterns
01
ReAct
Alternate between reasoning and acting — think before every tool call.
02
Plan & Execute
Generate the full task plan upfront, then execute each step sequentially.
03
Reflection
The agent reviews its own output and iterates until satisfied.
04
Tool Use
The agent decides which external tool to invoke and when.
Tier 2 Multi-Agent Orchestration 7 patterns
05
Orchestrator-Subagent
A coordinator breaks down goals and delegates to specialized workers.
06
Supervisor
Routes tasks, monitors outputs, and enforces quality gates.
07
Hierarchical Agents
Orchestrators manage other orchestrators across multiple levels.
08
Sequential Pipeline
Each agent completes its task and passes the result to the next.
09
Parallel Fan-Out/In
Tasks split across agents simultaneously, then results merge into one.
10
MapReduce
Distribute subtasks across many agents, then aggregate into a single output.
11
Debate / Adversarial
Agents argue opposing positions; a judge resolves the final answer.
Tier 3 Iterative & Feedback Loop 4 patterns
12
Evaluator-Optimizer
One agent generates, another scores; loop runs until quality is met.
13
Critic-Actor
A critic gives structured feedback; the actor refines until the bar is cleared.
14
Self-Healing
On failure, the agent diagnoses the error and retries with a corrected strategy.
15
HITL
A human steps in at defined checkpoints to approve, correct, or redirect.
